What is this exactly?

Earlier this year, I embarked on a personal project to develop a unique e-commerce platform. As a consultant, most of my work has been internally facing or under proprietary agreements, so I was eager to have a tangible project to discuss in interviews. It had to be complex enough to require a front end and back but not so complex that I was worried I couldn't finish it or get lost in it. I decided on e-commerce site because it seemed sensible, for someone who worked mainly in financial institutions. I wanted to think through the problems and constraints one would need to consider to make something that could function in the real world. The only problem is that I wanted to avoid managing a store and shipping goods to people for the sake of a resume-building exercise. "If only I could make a store that sold things that were guaranteed no one would actually want to buy." The thought made me laugh, so I made that the project's focus. The whole point of a store is that it's supposed to incentivize people to make purchases, so if you want the opposite, how does that change design? Which was intellectually engaging and funny enough to keep me motivated.

In college, some of my first coding projects involved managing and digitizing artifacts for museums. Through these projects, I learned about an early precursor to museums: the Cabinet of Curiousness. In short, they were collections of strange or fascinating curios that well-to-do eccentrics accumulated and then made open to the general public. So I got to work creating an e-commerce platform in a similar vein but in my own way. Instead of selling conventional products I'd take advantage of the fact that it was fictional. I began scouring the internet for oddest things one could purchase as well as inventing some with Photoshop's help (Asset design was another subject I was interested in learning) . The subject matter might 'seem' frivolous and silly. And, to be clear- it is. But I put a serious amount of effort and thought into an unserious thing. For example, sometimes online retailers intentionally implement poor design choices to motivate or obfuscate the decision to spend money on their goods and services. So, for my site, I decided to always let the customer know exactly how many items were inside their cart and how much money they were spending (taxes and delivery fees included) as opposed to hiding it until checkout. I used NGRX as a state management tool so that both pieces of information could be updated responsively. I finished earlier than I thought, so I started adding elements from various topics I was interested in and wanted to learn more about. I wanted an excuse to learn more about networking, so I created my internal network with private subnets on AWS and Docker. I was interested in DevOps, so I created an entire CI/CD pipeline to run, test, build, and deploy my code with a single button press.

Anyway, I hope you enjoy looking through my site. I had fun working on it. If you have any questions or would like to know more about me, you can find my contact info on my personal site HERE. Have a good day on the internet!
